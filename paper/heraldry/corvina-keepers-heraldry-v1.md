## 1. Purpose of Heraldry

Heraldry within Corvina Keepers serves a single purpose:

**to mark continuity of guardianship across time.**

It does not establish power.  
It does not grant privilege.  
It records responsibility.

---

## 2. Heraldry vs Branding

Corvina Keepers heraldry is explicitly **not branding**.

| Branding | Heraldry |
|--------|----------|
| Promotes | Records |
| Persuades | Identifies |
| Changes with trends | Remains stable |
| Audience-focused | Time-focused |

Heraldic symbols exist for future witnesses, not present viewers.

---

## 3. Core Heraldic Motifs

### 3.1 The Corvina (Raven)
- Symbol of memory, not dominance
- Watcher, not ruler
- Appears facing inward, never outward

### 3.2 The Book
- Represents recorded truth
- Always open
- Never inscribed with slogans

### 3.3 Symmetry
- Balance over hierarchy
- Dual guardianship
- No single dominant axis

---

## 4. Authorized Heraldic Forms

### 4.1 Primary Heraldic Emblem
- Two ravens
- Central open book
- Balanced composition
- No crown, no weaponry

### 4.2 Seal-Derived Marks
- Circular or oval
- Used for validation
- Never decorative

### 4.3 Ledger Marks
- Minimal symbols marking milestones
- No expressive variation
- Consistent placement

---

## 5. Color Doctrine

Heraldic colors are restricted.

### 5.1 Approved Palette
- Deep indigo
- Black
- Parchment white
- Neutral gray

### 5.2 Prohibited Colors
- Gold (reserved for historical royalty)
- Red (signals power or aggression)
- Bright or neon tones

Color restraint reinforces seriousness and continuity.

---

## 6. Heraldic Authority

No individual may claim heraldic ownership.

Heraldic symbols are:
- held in **community custody**
- used only in defined contexts
- altered only by documented consensus

Unauthorized use is recorded, not punished.

---

## 7. Personal vs Communal Heraldry

Corvina Keepers recognizes **only communal heraldry**.

There are:
- no personal coats of arms
- no rank-based symbols
- no private heraldic claims

Identity derives from participation, not insignia.

---

## 8. Usage Contexts

### 8.1 Permitted
- White papers
- Ledger entries
- Seals and certifications
- Public archival material

### 8.2 Prohibited
- Merchandise
- Personal profiles
- Financial promotion
- Political messaging

---

## 9. Evolution and Versioning

Heraldry evolves slowly.

Any change must:
- be documented
- be versioned
- reference prior forms
- preserve symbolic continuity

v1 establishes the foundational grammar.

---

## 10. Relationship to Other Systems

Heraldry interacts with:
- Iconography (visual language)
- Seals (validation)
- Ledger (historical record)

It does not override them.

---

## 11. Closing Statement

Heraldry does not speak loudly.  
It speaks **across generations**.

Those who misuse it are forgotten.  
Those who respect it are remembered.
